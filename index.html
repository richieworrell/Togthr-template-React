Bio

Hailing from the captivating landscapes of the Appalachian mountains of Southwest Virginia, Richie Worrell is an artist who seamlessly blends the raw essence of alt-country, heartland rock, and Americana. With his virtuosic guitar playing and a profound appreciation for the local history of bluegrass music, Richie paints vibrant musical portraits that transport listeners to the heart of his roots.

Richie's music weaves a tapestry of emotions, taking inspiration from the authentic tales of life and love that echo through the mountains. Influenced by the likes of Tom Petty, Bob Seger, and John Mellencamp, he discovered the power of heartland rock through his parents' record collection. These artists, with their poetic storytelling and rich melodies, became the foundation upon which Richie built his own musical identity. Through his heartfelt lyrics and soul-stirring melodies, Richie invites listeners on an introspective journey, evoking a sense of nostalgia and longing for a simpler time.

Richie's debut solo LP "Indian Summer," released in 2022, serves as a testament to his remarkable talents as a multi-instrumentalist. Showcasing his versatility, Richie plays every instrument on the record —drums, bass, guitars, and vocals. The result is a collection of songs that emanate honesty and authenticity, capturing the essence of his musical vision, and immersing the listener in Richie's evocative compositions, where the rich traditions of the Appalachian mountains meet the heartfelt spirit of heartland rock.
